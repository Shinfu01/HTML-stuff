"Huwag, huwag na muna nating pag-usapan"

Earlier today I was doing the pre-assignment that I was assigned to complete in September 15, 2024. If you're wondering, it isn't a school assignment. It's for something else that I am wanting to learn from. I had a choice to let the opportunity to go to waste or grab the opportunity to get better at programming.

Upon finishing, I didn't get to finish the entire problemset within the 2 hours. Afterwards, I went to facebook to go and check my school's page. When I went to facebook, I was greeted with something that, I, myself, have been wanting to join and compete in. The Philippine Mathematical Olympiad or the PMO. I checked the post and saw its a registration form for the school coordinators who wish to send 30 students to join and compete for the PMO.

ME: Should I send this to my math teacher? Am I even ready to compete for this? I feel like I am not yet capable to do such problems. I am only in the introductory-intermediate level of Algebra. I don't know anything about Geometry aside the angle, lines, areas, and circles. Combinatorics? I am not that confident with my skills yet along with Number Theory... haha..

After checking, I checked my school's page and there was no posts about school tomorrow. I then just went back to the problems and started upsolving all the problems that I couldn't solve. Moving to the 2nd and 3rd problem, they were so easy to solve, I only passed through the sample test cases and never got them AC'd decrementing my level by 1 (Now 7).

Fast forward a few hours, I got help how to solve this problem and asked for some hints, then I upsolved all of them. Suddenly, a few minutes go by and someone from the Mathematics discord server replied to my question yesterday about, 'How to get better at problem solving'. I was surprised to recieve a tip from someone that all I need is to learn about AMC 8-10 and do competition problems so that my problem solving skills will get better overtime by the time I get to participate in the PMO. I asked Sir Wu if doing AMC 8's and 10's will help me prepare better for PMO.

Me: Can the AMC 8 - 10 Problems and Book help me prepare for the PMO also?

Wu: Yes, though honestly, I think PMO areas might be a bit of a stretch with no contest math background, this year. My personal suggestion is to focus on the coding side for now, then add PMO next year to try to qualify for areas

Maybe along with preparing for the PMO, I might as well prepare myself for the coming college entrance exams next year.
